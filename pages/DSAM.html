Dynamic sensitivity analysis method (DSAM) improves parameter screening for earth system models
Xin Huang1,2, Yiqi Luo1,2,3, Wei Xue4,5
1 Center for Ecosystem Science and Society, Northern Arizona University, Flagstaff, AZ, USA
2 School of informatics, Computing, and Cyber Systems, Northern Arizona University, Flagstaff, AZ, USA
3 Department of Biological Sciences, Northern Arizona University, Flagstaff, AZ, USA
4 Department of Computer Science and Technology, Tsinghua University, Beijing, China
5 Department of Earth System Science, Ministry of Education Key Laboratory for Earth System Modelling, Tsinghua University, Beijing, China

Earth System Models (ESMs) have a large number of parameters, which introduces tremendous uncertainties in model performance. Sensitivity analysis (SA) is an essential step to identify the most influential parameters for model output. However, traditional sensitivity analysis evaluates parameters without considering changes in their interactions each time after less sensitive parameters are eliminated and consequently may miss some important parameters. In this study, we explored a dynamic sensitivity analysis method (DSAM) to improve parameter screening for ESMs. Starting from one most sensible parameter, DSAM iteratively evaluates the changes in group sensitivity and selects one more influential parameter to achieve the maximum variations. The method was used on two earth system models: community land model with Carnegie-Ames-Stanford Approach biogeochemical model (CLM-CASA) and Single Column Community Atmosphere Model (SCAM). Compared with traditional SA method, DSAM was able to identify more influential parameters, encompassing 2% - 50% more sensitivity information. Our study indicates that the DSAM is more effective than traditional SA methods to screen important parameters, especially for models with strong interactions among parameters.
