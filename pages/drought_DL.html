Drought forecasting in US based on a spatiotemporal prediction algorithm
Xin Huang1,2, Michael Gownloack1
1 Center for Ecosystem Science and Society, Northern Arizona University, Flagstaff, AZ, USA
2 School of informatics, Computing, and Cyber Systems, Northern Arizona University, Flagstaff, AZ, USA

Because of slow onset, drought events can easily evolve into a severe disaster without timely and enough perception from the society. A suitable drought forecasting technology is the central topic for drought mitigation. However, a major challenge for drought forecasting is the inability to simulate the spatiotemporal changes for hydro-climate processes in the globe. Here, we used monthly The Standardized Precipitation Evapotranspiration Index (SPEI) to reflect the drought conditions and adopted convGRU deep neural network to capture spatiotemporal correlations within the observed SPEI data sets. In the experiment of predicting the 2012 drought in US, this method proves a reasonable accuracy performance. To our best knowledge, this work is the first to apply convGRU network to drought prediction applications. This result highlights the feasibility of deep neural network in drought forecasting field.
